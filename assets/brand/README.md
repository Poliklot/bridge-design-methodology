# BRIDGE identity

The BRIDGE mark turns the acronym into one compact system:

- six modules represent **Breakpoints, Roles, Identity, Dependencies, Geometry, and Exceptions**;
- the alternating modules preserve their order while changing shape, echoing one stable identity across responsive states;
- the overall silhouette is an abstract **B** and a structural span rather than a literal bridge illustration.

## Core palette

| Token | Value | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| BRIDGE Ink | `#1E1E1E` | Primary mark and wordmark |
| BRIDGE Blue | `#0D99FF` | Transfer path and active intent |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Contrast modules and dark-surface wordmark |

## Usage

- Use `bridge-lockup-light.svg` on light surfaces and `bridge-lockup-dark.svg` on dark surfaces.
- Use `bridge-mark.svg` for app/plugin icons and square avatars.
- Use `bridge-mark-mono.svg` when only one color is available.
- Keep clear space around the mark equal to at least one internal module gap.
- Do not recolor individual modules, add gradients or effects, close the internal gaps, or redraw the mark as a literal bridge.
- Keep the tiled mark at least `16 × 16 px`; use the full lockup at widths of `120 px` or more.

The SVG files are the canonical sources. Raster exports are delivery assets only.
