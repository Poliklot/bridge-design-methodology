The identity is part of the methodology’s language, not a separate decorative layer.

## Meaning

The mark is built from six ordered modules:

1. **B** — Breakpoints
2. **R** — Roles
3. **I** — Identity
4. **D** — Dependencies
5. **G** — Geometry
6. **E** — Exceptions

The modules keep a shared rhythm while alternating between structural blocks and open-ended spans. Together they form an abstract **B**: one identity composed from explicit parts, readable at every size.

The mark deliberately avoids a literal bridge illustration. BRIDGE describes a transfer contract, so the symbol expresses stable structure and continuity rather than a place or object.

## Palette

| Token | Value | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| BRIDGE Ink | `#1E1E1E` | Structure, technical truth, primary wordmark |
| BRIDGE Blue | `#0D99FF` | Declared intent, active transfer path |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Contrast and open space |

The primary mark uses only these three colors. Product status colors such as error, warning, and draft are interface semantics and must not be introduced into the logo.

## Rules

- Use the complete color mark whenever the surface supports it.
- Use the one-color mark for stamps, small technical diagrams, or restricted production.
- Keep clear space equal to at least one internal module gap.
- Minimum size: `16 × 16 px` for the mark; `120 px` wide for the lockup.
- Preserve module order, spacing, corner geometry, and the alternating blue/white rhythm.
- Do not add gradients, shadows, outlines, extra labels, or a literal bridge arch.
- Do not use the six modules as unrelated decoration; their order carries meaning.
